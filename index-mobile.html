Welcome!

The Erzya language is a Uralic language belonging to the Volga-Finnic subgroup, also known as the Mordvinic subgroup, of the Finno-Ugric languages. It is closely related to the Moksha language, and in the past, these two were considered dialects of the so-called 'Mordvin language'. In the 2021 Russian census, around 50,000 people identified Erzya as their native language, with the majority living in the Republic of Mordovia. Erzya is one of the official languages of the Republic of Mordovia, alongside Russian and Moksha. While the official Erzya alphabet is identical to the Russian alphabet, scientific literature often uses various Latin-based systems to represent the language.

Paročisë sovado!

Erźań keleś te suomiń-ugrań semijanť suomiń-ravoń (lijaks meremga: mordvań) taradonteń sovića uraloń keľ. Sonenzë vesemede malaviks keleś — mokšonseś. Ikele sïnst kavonest keltneń loviź vejsëń isťa lemdeź „mordvań keleń“ dialektëks. 2021 ijeń vese Rasejeń lovnomasonť malav 50 000 lomanť teškstïź erźań kelenť eś keleks, sïndest lamot eriť Mordovija Respublikaso. Keleś ašti mastoroń keleks Mordovijaso, ruzoń dï mokšoń keltneń jutkso. Pravań koŕas kemekstaź śormadoma kojezë ruzoń konďamo, teke marto naukań literaturaso sejedstë noldtneviť tevs eŕva kodamo lija, latinëń alfavitënť langs nežedeź sistemat.
