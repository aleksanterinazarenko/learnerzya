Welcome!


The Erzya language is a Uralic language belonging to the Volga-Finnic or alternatively the Mordvinic subgroup of the Finno-Ugric languages. The Erzya people speak this language. In the 2010 census conducted in Russia, 36,726 people identified Erzya as their native language, the majority of whom live in the Republic of Mordovia. The Erzya language is closely related to the Moksha language. In the past, these two languages were considered dialects of a common Mordvin language.

Erzya is an official language in the Republic of Mordovia, alongside Russian and Moksha. The official alphabet of the Erzya language is identical to the Russian alphabet, but in scientific literature, many other Latin-based systems are often used.

Erzäń keľ te suomiń-ugrań semijanť suomiń-ravoń (lijaks: mordvań) taradonteń sovavtovicä uraloń keľ. Erzäń kelsë kortïť erzät. Rossijaso erzätnede 2010 ijeń lovnomasonť tekšstïź erzäń kelenť koda tirineks 36 726 lomať, sïndest lamotne eriť Mordovija Respublikaso. Erzäń kelenteń vesemede malaviks keleś — mokšoń keľ. Ikele keltneń loviź vejke mordvań keleń dialektëks.

Erzäń keleś ašti eslempeleń keleks Mordovijaso, ruzoń dï mokšoń keltneń marto. Eslempeleń śormadoma kojeś — ruzoń kirilicä, teke škasto naukań literaturaso sejedstë noldtneviť tevs lamo lija, latinicä langs nežedeź sistemat.
